Completing the square
  • Y11 (current)
1. Write the following in the form \((x+p)^2 + q\)
a) \(x^2+4x\)
b) \(x^2-10x+7\)
c) \(x^2+14x-9\)
2. Solve the following quadratic equations (by first completing the square if necessary)
a) \((x-7)^2 - 5 = 0\)
b) \(x^2 - 3 = 6x\)
c) \(6x - 7 - x^2 = 0\)
3. Write the following in the form \(r(x+s)^2+t\)
a) \(2x^2+8x+13\)
b) \(2x^2 - 12x + 17\)
c) \(3x^2 - 6x - 1\)
d) \(5x^2 + 3x + 8\)
e) \(-x^2 - 2x + 7\)
f) \(-2x^2 + 3x - 1\)
4. Given that \(x^2+px+4 = (x+q)^2 - 5\), find the value of the constants \(p\) and \(q\)
5. Given that \(x^2+4x+m = (x+2)^2+3m\), find the value of \(m\)
6. Sketch the graph of \(y = x^2 - 4x - 5\). Hence, find a) the points of intersection with the x-axis, b) the point of intersection with the y-axis, and c) the coordinates of the minimum point.
7. Find the coordinates of the minimum point of the graph \(y = x^2-x-7\). Hence find the equation of the line of symmetry of this curve.
8. Write \(x^2+6x+13\) in the form \((x+a)^2+b\). Write down the coordinates of the turning point of the graph \(y = x^2+6x+13\), and hence state the number of solutions to the equation \(x^2+6x+13=0\), giving a reason for your answer.
9. The solutions to the equation \(2x^2+px+q = 0\) are \(\displaystyle x = 2 \pm \sqrt{\dfrac{11}{2}}\). Find the values of \(p\) and \(q\).
10. By completing the square on the equation \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), prove that the solution of a general quadratic equation is given by \(x = \dfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)
